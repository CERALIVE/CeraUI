## Design Context

### Users
Live streamers, broadcasters, and content creators ranging from professional broadcast engineers to passionate hobbyists. They use CeraUI to configure CeraLive streaming encoder hardware — managing network connections (cellular modems, Wi-Fi, Ethernet), streaming pipelines, video encoding, and audio settings. They operate in every context: high-pressure field broadcasts, controlled studio environments, and casual home setups. They need an interface that works flawlessly on mobile in the field, on tablets at events, and on desktop in studios.

### Brand Personality
**Robust, Intuitive, Professional** — with the precision and energy of broadcast engineering. The interface should feel like a control room built by people who live streaming. It communicates confidence and trust: when a user configures their encoder before a live broadcast, the UI should reassure them that everything is under control. Not decorative, not sterile — sharp and purposeful, like professional broadcast equipment.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Broadcast Control Room** — the precision of professional broadcast equipment meets the energy of a live production environment. Cool, high-contrast, and alive. Every element earns its place.

- **Tone**: Dark & techy. Clean information density where needed (this is a tool, not a marketing site), sharp visual language with high contrast, confident use of space.
- **Color**: Cool slate neutrals (hue 215-222) for backgrounds and surfaces. Electric cyan (#11C4D4) is the primary accent — a neon-tech glow that reads like an active HUD indicator against the cool backdrop. Deep blue-black dark mode (#0b0f1a) where cyan glows at full neon intensity (#1FE7F9), clean cool-white light mode (#f5f7fa). The electric cyan against deep blue-black creates the visual tension of a heads-up display on broadcast hardware.
- **Typography**: Distinctive but highly readable. Geometric warmth for headings (Plus Jakarta Sans), clear technical precision for body text and data (JetBrains Mono for monospace). Both served locally.
- **Anti-references**: Must NOT look like generic AI-generated UI (purple gradients, glassmorphism everywhere), must NOT look like a default shadcn template with a color swap, must NOT be bloated with decorative elements that serve no purpose, must NOT use warm stone/beige neutrals (too muted, lacks energy).
- **References**: The confidence of Blackmagic Design software, the clarity of Linear, the energy of broadcast monitoring interfaces — adapted for a hardware configuration context.
- **Theme**: Both light and dark modes, both treated as first-class citizens. Dark mode is the hero — deep blue-black with electric neon cyan accents.
- **Responsive**: Must excel across mobile (field use), tablet (event management), and desktop (studio configuration). Not "shrink the desktop" — genuinely adapted layouts per context.

### Design Principles
1. **Earned Confidence**: Every visual choice should make users feel their stream is in good hands. Status indicators are clear, actions are unambiguous, feedback is immediate.
2. **Tool, Not Template**: This is a professional instrument. Information density is appropriate, not everything needs a card wrapper, and the layout serves the workflow rather than decoration.
3. **Sharp Precision**: Cool neutrals and electric cyan create a high-contrast, neon-tech feel. The electric cyan primary against cool slate surfaces is intentional — it draws the eye to actions and status, like glowing indicators on a heads-up display.
4. **Responsive by Design**: Mobile, tablet, and desktop are distinct experiences designed for their context — not breakpoint-shrunken copies of each other.
5. **Opinionated Simplicity**: Strong aesthetic point of view executed with restraint. One well-placed accent beats ten decorative elements.
