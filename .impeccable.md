## Design Context

### Users
Live streamers, broadcasters, and content creators ranging from professional broadcast engineers to passionate hobbyists. They use CeraUI to configure CeraLive streaming encoder hardware — managing network connections (cellular modems, Wi-Fi, Ethernet), streaming pipelines, video encoding, and audio settings. They operate in every context: high-pressure field broadcasts, controlled studio environments, and casual home setups. They need an interface that works flawlessly on mobile in the field, on tablets at events, and on desktop in studios.

### Brand Personality
**Robust, Intuitive, Professional** — with a touch of artisan craftsmanship. The interface should feel like a precision tool built by people who care deeply about streaming. It communicates confidence and trust: when a user configures their encoder before a live broadcast, the UI should reassure them that everything is under control. Not flashy, not sterile — purposeful and considered, like a well-made instrument.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Broadcast Atelier** — the precision of professional broadcast equipment meets the warmth of a craftsman's workshop. Opinionated but not bloated. Every element earns its place.

- **Tone**: Professional-artisan. Clean information density where needed (this is a tool, not a marketing site), warm and considered visual language, confident use of space.
- **Color**: The CeraLive logo uses warm orange (#EB4F27). The UI palette should relate to this — warm neutrals, the logo orange as a primary accent, with a cool complement for secondary/status contexts. Not cold blue-gray defaults.
- **Typography**: Distinctive but highly readable. Geometric warmth for headings, clear technical precision for body text and data.
- **Anti-references**: Must NOT look like generic AI-generated UI (cyan-on-dark, purple gradients, glassmorphism everywhere), must NOT look like a default shadcn template with a color swap, must NOT be bloated with decorative elements that serve no purpose.
- **References**: The confidence of Blackmagic Design software, the clarity of Linear, the warmth of Stripe's dashboard — adapted for a hardware configuration context.
- **Theme**: Both light and dark modes, both treated as first-class citizens.
- **Responsive**: Must excel across mobile (field use), tablet (event management), and desktop (studio configuration). Not "shrink the desktop" — genuinely adapted layouts per context.

### Design Principles
1. **Earned Confidence**: Every visual choice should make users feel their stream is in good hands. Status indicators are clear, actions are unambiguous, feedback is immediate.
2. **Tool, Not Template**: This is a professional instrument. Information density is appropriate, not everything needs a card wrapper, and the layout serves the workflow rather than decoration.
3. **Warm Precision**: Professional doesn't mean cold. Typography, color, and spacing carry warmth and intentionality that distinguish this from generic tooling.
4. **Responsive by Design**: Mobile, tablet, and desktop are distinct experiences designed for their context — not breakpoint-shrunken copies of each other.
5. **Opinionated Simplicity**: Strong aesthetic point of view executed with restraint. One well-placed accent beats ten decorative elements.
